Sombrero Motivation

We want to fit any multivariate surface as the solution to a total variation problem. One complex surface that we can use for demonstration is a sombrero, which is given by \[ y = f_{som}(x_1,x_2)=\frac{\sin\left(15\left(x_1^2+x_2^2\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\right)}{\left(25x_1^2+25x_2^2\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}}. \] For aesthetic purposes, we restrict the domain such that \(x_1, x_2 \in [-1,1]\) (sombreros are not actually too wavy). In Figure 1, we plot the sombrero with and without noise at a sample size of \(N=900\) to give a sense of what we are aiming to fit:

# Note that we use gen_mesh() in MultivarTV package to generate function.
dat <- seq(-1+0.0001,1-0.0001,length.out = 5)
mat <- cbind(dat,dat)
m <- matrix(rep(30,2),ncol=1)
dataMesh <- gen_mesh(mat,m,NULL )
y <- sombrero(dataMesh[,1],dataMesh[,2])
set.seed(117)
ynoisy <- y + rnorm(length(y),0,0.1)

Figure 1. Sombrero function without noise (a) and with noise (b).

Let us fit the noisy sombrero using a total variation solution. We do not expect an aesthetically pleasing fit, since we find the best piecewise constant fit to the noisy sombrero. Typically, when we want to fit wavy surfaces, other methods come to mind like the thin plate spline. In this exercise, we will compare a total variation fit against a thin plate spline. Based on Figure 2, we indeed get a jagged sombrero for our total variation solution. In Figure 3, we see the thin plate spline solution, which is much more smooth and perhaps introduces too much wiggliness along the ridges of the sombrero. When we increase the noise (from \(\sigma=0.1\) to \(\sigma=0.5\)), thin plate splines behave similarly as before (Figure 5), while the total variation solution has more regularization (Figure 4). This contrast illustrates how a denoising solution will tend to behave in the presence of large amounts of noise, i.e. more regularity will be introduced in the solution. However, this does not mean that the fit is necessarily better or worse. Next, we investigate how the total variation solution compares to the thin plate spline in simulation.

set.seed(117)
mym <- matrix(rep(sqrt(length(y)),2),ncol=1)
tvmod <- mvtv(dataMesh,ynoisy,mym,verbose=FALSE)
plotFits(tvmod,adddata = FALSE)

Figure 2. Total variation solution for noisy sombrero.

fit <- Tps(dataMesh,ynoisy)
out.p <- predictSurface(fit, xy = c(1,2))
plot.surface(out.p, type="p")

Figure 3. Thin plate spline solution for noisy sombrero.

set.seed(117)
ynoisy2 <- y + rnorm(length(y),0,0.5)

set.seed(117)
tvmod2 <- mvtv(dataMesh,ynoisy2,mym,verbose=FALSE)
plotFits(tvmod,adddata = FALSE)

Figure 4. Total variation solution for noisier sombrero.

fit2 <- Tps(dataMesh,ynoisy2)
out.p2 <- predictSurface(fit2, xy = c(1,2))
plot.surface(out.p2, type="p")

Figure 5. Thin plate spline solution for noisier sombrero.
